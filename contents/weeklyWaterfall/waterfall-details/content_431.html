While COVID 19 rage is slowing down, many are still concerned about their workplace sanity or want to confirm whether employers are taking necessary steps if someone tests positive.

The good news is, as per New COVID-19 Exposure and Notification Law (AB 685), all private and public employers who will receive notice of potential exposure to COVID-19 must provide written notice to all employees who may have been exposed. They should also provide COVID-19 benefits-related information like worker's compensation, COVID-19-related leave, and paid sick leave, as well as the employer's anti-discrimination and anti-retaliation policies within one day.

If an "imminent hazard" is found, Cal/OSHA can even shut down the workplace to minimize the hazard and ensure employees' safety.
