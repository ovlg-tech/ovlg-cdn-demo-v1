For many students and their parents, the college became significantly more expensive due to the Covid-19 epidemic and the ensuing economic crisis. As a result, they've become even more reliant on the Free Application for Federal Student Aid, or FAFSA, for their children's college funding.

On Friday, Oct. 1, the FAFSA filing season for the upcoming 2022-23 school year began. Through FAFSA, you can apply for state and institutional aid in addition to federal aid. Over a dozen states give awards on a first-come, first-served basis, particularly for programs with limited finances. As a result, the earlier pupils file, the better. In October, students who file their FAFSA are often awarded more than twice as much grant money as those who file later.

So, if you want to maximize your aid eligibility, file your FAFSA as soon as possible.
