Yes, you've read it right. As per the final rules (issued by the IRS), non-profit hospitals can't indulge into aggressive debt collection practices unless they determine if a patient is eligible for financial assistance. Typical aggressive debt collection practices include:

(a) Wage garnishment (b) Assigning accounts to debt collection agencies (c) Reporting the debt to credit reports (d) Engaging into collection practices in emergency rooms

Before the charitable hospital gets into involved into aggressive debt collection practices, they need to inform the patient about financial aids.
