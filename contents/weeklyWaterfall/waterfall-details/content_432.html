Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021 (CAA 2021) was passed on 21st Dec '20 and has brought some temporary changes in the bankruptcy code that will last up to 27th Dec '22.

Under Section 1001(b), Courts may grant Chapter 13 discharges to debtors even after they default on up to three monthly residentiary mortgage payments after 13th Mar 2020, as a result of COVID-19. This rule is also applicable for the debtors who include residential property in a "cure and maintain" plan and participate in a qualifying loan modification or forbearance.

If you have defaulted on mortgage payments and yet want to obtain a Chapter 13 discharge, make sure to establish that the missed payments were due to covid19 related financial hardship.
