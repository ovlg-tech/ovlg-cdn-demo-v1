Our policymakers' attention has been drawn back to our healthcare system due to this pandemic. As a result, several new laws were entered into effect this year.

Connecticut House Bill 6003, which went into effect on January 1, 2021, is one such law.

Under this law, diabetes patients in Connecticut can inform their pharmacist if they have less than a week of medicine and other diabetes-related supplies. After confirming with Connecticut's emergency drug monitoring program, the pharmacist can get these people the supplies they require.

On January 1, 2022, a new provision of this law will take effect. As a result, a 30-day supply of insulin for anyone with state-regulated insurance will cost no more than $25 next year. According to the Connecticut state health department, diabetes is the seventh-highest cause of death, affecting roughly 10%. This law is supposed to assist people in controlling their diabetes at a low cost.
