A new layer of protection has been added for Californian consumers to save them from unlawful debt collection practices. Beginning on January 1, 2022, anyone engaged in the debt collection business in California has to be licensed under the Debt Collection Licensing Act (SB 908). Licensed entities must also develop compliance-focused policies and procedures, maintain surety bonds, file annual reports, and undergo periodic examinations. The Act also amended existing state debt collection laws.

The exemption will be available for some entities licensed by the California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI). The application will be available through the Nationwide Multistate Licensing System (“NMLS”) website. DFPI has declared that only the applicants who submit applications on or before December 31, 2021, may continue to operate in California while their application status is pending.
