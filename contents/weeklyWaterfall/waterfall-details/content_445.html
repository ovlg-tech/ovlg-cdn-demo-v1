Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS) is a progressive neurodegenerative disease that destroys the brain and spinal cord nerve cells. Sadly, this condition has no recognized cure. So, govt. financial assistance helps these individuals to prolong their lives by focusing on what matters most.

The ALS Disability Insurance Access Act of 2019 was signed into law by the president last December. In March 2021, the president signed S 579 into public law 117-3 to make a technical correction to the ALS Disability Insurance Access Act of 2019. S 579 has eliminated the five-month waiting period for the Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) benefits for ALS individuals approved on or after July 23, 2020.

The rapid and severe course of ALS robs people of their careers and employer-based insurance during their most difficult times. Unlike some other rare diseases, this disease does not reverse course. Hence the waiting period used to impede ALS individuals' access to a financial lifeline. Thanks to the current law, they can now bypass it successfully.
