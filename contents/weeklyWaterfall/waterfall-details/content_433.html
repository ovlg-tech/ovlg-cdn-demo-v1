Since 2014 Americans are using the marketplace while buying their health insurance. Getting eligible for premium tax credits/subsidies makes coverage more affordable, but eligibility depends on the income limit.

People with household incomes greater than 400% of the poverty line were previously ineligible for premium tax credits. But with the American Rescue Plan 2021 (Section 9661), that subsidy cliff has been eliminated for 2021 and 2022. Under the new rule, people earning 400% of the poverty line or more are only required to spend 8.5% of their household income to purchase the benchmark plan. This new rule is beneficial, especially for those who are older and/or live in an area where premiums are expensive.
