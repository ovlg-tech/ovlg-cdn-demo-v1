In April, New York allocated $2.1 billion from its state budget to compensate undocumented immigrants for the COVID-19 pandemic. The payment will be exclusively available for those workers who have not received any federal benefit due to their immigrant status. The program is known as The excluded worker fund, and it can also benefit the other low-income workers who have not received any pandemic relief measure yet.

The Excluded Worker Fund can provide eligible recipients with payments of $15,600 (tier 1 benefit) or $3,200 (tier 2 benefit), depending on their eligibility. In NYC, the program is expected to benefit nearly 290,000 people.

To be eligible, candidates must have lived in New York prior to March 27, 2020, and demonstrate that they lost income after February 2020 due to the pandemic. Acceptable forms of identification include a state-issued driver's license or IDNYC card, a passport, and marriage or birth certificates.
