Are you thinking about filing for bankruptcy or already filed one? Here's a piece of good news for you. President Biden has enacted COVID-19 Bankruptcy Relief Extension Act on (H.R 1651) on 27th March to extend provisions providing financially distressed consumers and small businesses greater access to bankruptcy relief.

For the individual consumers who are filing (filed) for chapter 7 or 13, federal COVID-19 relief-related payments won't be considered income. Also, the calculation of disposable income to confirm a chapter 13 plan will not include coronavirus-related payments. In chapter 13, you can modify your payment plan if you face any financial hardship due to pandemics.
