A number of new labor rules are being enacted in California, and they will take effect next year. Governor Newsom recently signed AB 1033, which expands the California Family Rights Act (CFRA) leaves. Under this law, employers must provide eligible employees with 12 weeks of job-protected time off every year to care for their in-laws who are in critical medical condition.

Currently, CFRA allows 12 weeks of job-protected leave each year for employees to bond with a new child or care for urgent medical needs. On the other hand, parents-in-law will be included in this list as a result of AB 1033. This extension presently excludes private firms with fewer than 5 employees. AB 1033 also provides a mediation pilot program for firms with 5 to 19 employees.

In general, an employee must have worked for the employer for a minimum of one year and 1,250 hours in the 12 months before requesting this increased job-protected time off under the CFRA.
